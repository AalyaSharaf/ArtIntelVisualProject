About

As we know, music comes in many different forms. You hear a song, and there is this immediate classification or differentiation that you make in your head. A classical piece sounds extremely different to a country one. Your brains have been wired to understand the differences between them, but what happens when we take what is auditory and turn it into something visual? Will our brains be able to distinguish as easily?


I put over 60 prompts and song titles and watched what unfolded before me. These songs fell under 4 major categories: Arabic, CLassical, Disney, and Rock. It is a limited scope, but I plan to expand on it myself since this is a topic that intrigues me. There are 3 main things I wanted to explore with this project. The first is whether the song titles that fall under the same category will develop images that could be identifiable as “falling under the same group”. Meaning how similar or different will the results be? Along the same lines, my second point was to compare images of songs from different genres and seeing if anything overlaps. Finally, I wanted to explore whether or not the brain would be able to distinguish what song falls under what category based on JUST an image. Again, the whole concept of turning auditory into visual.
